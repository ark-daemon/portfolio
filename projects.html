Knowledge Base · Glossary
  • Support & Resistance Price Action

    Price levels where an asset historically reverses or pauses. Support is a floor where buying pressure exceeds selling; resistance is a ceiling where selling pressure exceeds buying.

  • Risk/Reward Ratio Risk Management

    The ratio of potential profit to potential loss on a trade. A 1:3 RR means risking $1 to target $3 in gain — a core filter before entering any position.

  • Liquidity Sweep Market Structure

    A sharp price move that clears clustered stop-loss orders above a high or below a low before reversing in the opposite direction. Often engineered by larger participants to fill their own orders.

  • HTF Bias Multi-Timeframe

    The directional trend established on a higher timeframe (daily or weekly) used to filter lower-timeframe entries. Trades taken in the direction of HTF bias have a statistically higher probability.

  • Funding Rate Derivatives

    A periodic payment exchanged between long and short traders on perpetual futures contracts to keep the price tethered to spot. Extremely positive funding signals crowded longs and potential squeeze risk.

  • Order Block Smart Money

    The last opposing candle before a significant impulsive move, believed to mark where institutional orders were placed. Price often returns to these zones before continuing in the original direction.
